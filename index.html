About

I am a mechanical engineering undergraduate with strong interests in manufacturing-focused design, system integration, and computational analysis. My work emphasizes building engineering systems that can be fabricated, assembled, tested, and iterated under real-world constraints.

Through international UAV competitions, research-driven simulation projects, and industry-oriented work, I have gained experience where design decisions must balance manufacturability, cost, reliability, and deployment timelines — not just analytical performance.

I am particularly motivated by environments that value hands-on experimentation and learning-by-doing, where engineering intuition is developed through repeated design–build–test cycles.

Selected Engineering Work

  • Low-Cost Modular UAV Platform: Designed and fabricated a modular UAV architecture focused on design-for-manufacturing, rapid assembly, and structural robustness.
  • Agricultural Spraying & Monitoring UAV: Developed a payload-capable UAV with emphasis on stability, modular integration, and field-ready manufacturing.
  • CFD Optimization of Submersible Pump & Blower Systems: Applied CFD to study internal flow behavior and pressure rise, guiding dimension-driven geometry improvements.
  • Storm-Resistant UAV Systems: Combined CFD, structural analysis, and manufacturing constraints to evaluate performance under adverse operating conditions.

Detailed design decisions, analysis workflows, and outcomes are documented in the Projects and Competitions sections.

Research Focus

My research interests center on simulation-driven design and computational modeling. I have worked with OpenFOAM on numerical modeling of radiofrequency ablation, exploring how physics-based solvers and data-driven methods can support faster, patient-specific insights.

I am particularly interested in how computational tools can inform practical engineering decisions when integrated with manufacturing and system constraints.
