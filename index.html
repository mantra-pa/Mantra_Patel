About

I am a mechanical engineering undergraduate interested in advanced manufacturing, computational design, and system-level engineering. My work focuses on designing, analyzing, and fabricating engineering systems under real-world constraints such as manufacturability, cost, reliability, and time.

Through UAV design competitions, research-driven simulation work, and industry-oriented projects, I have worked on problems where engineering decisions must be validated through building, testing, and iteration — not just analysis.

I am particularly motivated by environments that emphasize hands-on experimentation, analytical rigor, and learning by doing, where engineering intuition is developed through repeated design–build–test cycles.

Selected Engineering Work

  • Low-Cost Modular UAV Platform: Designed and fabricated a modular UAV focused on manufacturability and rapid iteration, reducing structural weight and assembly complexity.
  • Agricultural Spraying & Monitoring UAV: Engineered a payload-capable UAV with emphasis on stability under variable loading and field-ready manufacturing.
  • CFD Optimization of Submersible Pump Casing: Used CFD to refine internal geometry, reducing hydraulic losses and improving flow uniformity.
  • Storm-Resistant UAV Design: Combined CFD and FEA to evaluate aerodynamic stability, structural robustness, and thermal considerations under adverse conditions.

Detailed engineering decisions, analysis methods, and outcomes are documented in the Projects and Competitions sections.

Research Focus

My research interests lie in computational modeling and simulation-driven design. As a CFD & OpenFOAM research intern, I have worked on solver development, numerical efficiency, and the integration of Physics-Informed Neural Networks (PINNs) to accelerate convergence in complex simulations.

I am particularly interested in how computational tools can be combined with physical intuition to guide real engineering design decisions.
