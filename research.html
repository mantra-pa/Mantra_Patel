Effect of Close Proximity of Rotating Propellers on Multirotor UAV Performance

Conference Paper | FMFP 2025

Conference: 12th International and 52nd National Conference on Fluid Mechanics and Fluid Power (FMFP 2025)

Venue: Nirma University, Ahmedabad, India

Dates: 19‚Äì21 December 2025

This research investigates aerodynamic interaction effects arising from close propeller spacing in multirotor UAV configurations. The study focuses on wake interference, induced flow interaction, and resulting performance variation, with direct relevance to compact UAV propulsion design.

Research Highlights:

  • Analyzed propeller-propeller aerodynamic interference in close proximity configurations
  • Quantified performance degradation due to wake interaction effects
  • Developed design guidelines for optimal propeller spacing in multirotor systems
  • Validated findings through experimental and computational methods

Computational Modeling of Radiofrequency Ablation (OpenFOAM)

Developed numerical models to study bioheat transfer and thermal lesion formation during radiofrequency ablation. The work emphasized patient-specific parameterization and minimizing unintended thermal damage.

Key Contributions:

  • Implemented physics-based thermal models using OpenFOAM framework
  • Studied heat transfer in biological tissues during RFA procedures
  • Optimized treatment parameters to minimize collateral damage
  • Validated models against clinical and experimental data
