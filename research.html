Effect of Close Proximity of Rotating Propellers on Multirotor UAV Performance

Accepted for presentation at FMFP 2025 • Aerodynamic analysis of multirotor UAVs

Paper Information

Conference: FMFP 2025 (Nirma University, Ahmedabad, India)

Research Motivation

In compact multirotor UAV configurations, propellers often operate in close proximity due to geometric and packaging constraints. Such arrangements can significantly alter flow structures, wake interactions, and induced velocities, impacting thrust generation and efficiency.

Objective

The objective of this work was to study the aerodynamic performance variation of multirotor UAVs due to close propeller spacing, with emphasis on understanding interference effects and their implications for UAV design.

Methodology

  • Aerodynamic analysis of two rotating propellers at varying spacing
  • Evaluation of thrust and performance metrics
  • Study of wake interaction and flow interference effects
  • Comparison of performance trends across configurations

Key Findings

  • Close propeller proximity leads to measurable aerodynamic interaction effects
  • Wake interference alters thrust characteristics and efficiency
  • Compact designs involve trade-offs between packaging and performance
