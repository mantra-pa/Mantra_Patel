Research Overview

Compact multirotor UAV configurations often require propellers to operate in close proximity due to geometric and packaging constraints. Such arrangements lead to aerodynamic interaction effects arising from wake interference and modified induced flow fields.

This research investigates how close propeller spacing influences thrust generation and aerodynamic performance in multirotor UAV systems. The study focuses on identifying performance trends and understanding trade-offs between vehicle compactness and propulsion efficiency.

Research Objective

The objective of this work was to analyze the effect of close proximity between two rotating propellers on the aerodynamic performance of a multirotor UAV, with emphasis on thrust variation and interaction-induced performance changes.

Key Findings

  • Aerodynamic Interaction Effects

    Close propeller proximity introduces measurable aerodynamic interaction effects that significantly impact thrust generation and efficiency.

  • Wake Interference

    Wake interference alters thrust characteristics and efficiency, with effects becoming more pronounced at closer spacing distances.

  • Design Trade-offs

    Compact UAV designs involve trade-offs between packaging constraints and propulsion performance‚Äîsmaller footprints come at the cost of aerodynamic efficiency.

Research Significance

This research addresses a critical challenge in multirotor UAV design: balancing vehicle compactness with propulsion efficiency. As UAVs become smaller and more portable, understanding propeller interaction effects becomes essential for maintaining performance and mission capability.
