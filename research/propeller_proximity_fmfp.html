Research Overview

Compact multirotor UAV configurations often require propellers to operate in close proximity due to geometric and packaging constraints. Such arrangements lead to aerodynamic interaction effects arising from wake interference and modified induced flow fields.

This research investigates how close propeller spacing influences thrust generation and aerodynamic performance in multirotor UAV systems. The study focuses on identifying performance trends and understanding trade-offs between vehicle compactness and propulsion efficiency.

Objective

The objective of this work was to analyze the effect of close proximity between two rotating propellers on the aerodynamic performance of a multirotor UAV, with emphasis on thrust variation and interaction-induced performance changes.

Methodology

  • Aerodynamic analysis of two rotating propellers at varying spacing distances
  • Evaluation of thrust and performance metrics under proximity effects
  • Analysis of wake interaction and induced flow behavior
  • Comparison of performance trends across multiple configurations

Key Findings

  • Close propeller proximity introduces measurable aerodynamic interaction effects
  • Wake interference alters thrust characteristics and efficiency
  • Compact UAV designs involve trade-offs between packaging constraints and propulsion performance

My Contribution

I contributed to the aerodynamic analysis and performance evaluation of closely spaced rotating propellers, including configuration setup, analysis of thrust variation trends, and interpretation of wake interaction effects relevant to multirotor UAV design.
