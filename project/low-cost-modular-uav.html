Project Overview

This project was developed to address a recurring challenge observed during UAV development within Team Arrow: meaningful testing was frequently delayed until final deadlines because full-scale UAV manufacturing was expensive, time-intensive, and early failures often resulted in crashes and rework.

Challenge: High-cost composite UAVs limited early testing opportunities, resulting in delayed validation and increased risk of late-stage failures.

Solution: A low-cost, modular plywood platform enabling rapid experimentation before committing to expensive composite manufacturing.

Design & CAD Development

The airframe architecture was developed with a strong emphasis on manufacturability and modularity rather than minimum weight or peak aerodynamic efficiency.

Manufacturing & Fabrication

Structural components were manufactured using laser-cut plywood, enabling rapid turnaround, low cost, and repeatable fabrication without specialized tooling.

Key Manufacturing Advantages:

  • Rapid Production

    Same-day turnaround from CAD to physical parts using laser cutting services.

  • Cost Efficiency

    Material cost less than 10% of composite equivalent, enabling multiple prototypes.

  • No Specialized Tools

    Eliminated need for molds, jigs, or curing equipment required for composite fabrication.

  • Design Iteration

    Low cost enabled aggressive testing and rapid design improvements.

Assembly & Modularity

A jigsaw-style modular assembly was used to allow fast assembly, easy repair, and quick configuration changes without adhesives or complex fixturing.

Tool-Free Assembly

Components snap together without adhesives, enabling rapid disassembly for transport and storage.

Field Repairability

Damaged sections can be replaced in minutes using spare parts, minimizing downtime.

Configuration Flexibility

Modular design allows testing different wing positions, payloads, and subsystem arrangements.

Testing & Validation

The platform was used to validate flight behavior, control strategies, payload integration, and assembly logic before scaling designs to composite airframes.

Validation Objectives:

  • Flight Characteristics

    Validated stability, control response, and handling qualities early in development.

  • Subsystem Integration

    Tested avionics, power systems, and payload mounting before final design freeze.

  • Assembly Procedures

    Identified interference issues and optimized assembly sequences for competition deployment.

  • Risk Reduction

    Eliminated costly surprises by catching integration issues in low-cost prototypes.

Engineering Insight

This project demonstrated how manufacturing-aware design can accelerate learning and reduce system-level risk by enabling early and frequent testing. Rather than optimizing a single aircraft, the focus shifted to designing a development platform that supports iteration under real-world constraints.

Key Learnings:
