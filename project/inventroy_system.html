Problem Context

In small manufacturing environments, inventory and procurement decisions are often reactive and manually driven, relying on continuous vendor follow-ups. This leads to higher procurement costs, delayed replenishment, and increased risk of production downtime.

Project Objective

The objective of this project was to design a system-level workflow that integrates inventory tracking, demand estimation, and market-aware procurement timing to reduce costs while ensuring uninterrupted manufacturing.

System Flow & Decision Logic

When a production order is placed, the system generates the bill of materials (BOM) and checks current inventory availability. Required quantities are calculated, and demand is estimated using an EOQ-based approach.

Market prices are forecasted in parallel, and procurement costs are evaluated for both immediate purchasing and forecasted price conditions. If forecasted procurement costs are lower, required inventory is locked for baseline manufacturing while procurement is delayed.

Once favorable market conditions are achieved, the procurement order is automatically triggered. Safety stock levels are calculated using distributor network variability, and projected future pump prices are displayed to support distributor-side ordering decisions.

Why This Matters

In manufacturing systems, procurement timing often has a greater impact on operational cost and reliability than inventory size alone. This project demonstrates how integrating demand estimation, market forecasting, and decision logic can reduce procurement costs without increasing inventory holding or risking production continuity.

The system illustrates how data-driven decision-making can replace reactive vendor coordination, improving supply resilience and operational robustness.

Key Learnings

  • System-level thinking is critical for manufacturing operations
  • Procurement timing is as important as inventory quantity
  • Market awareness can be integrated into engineering decision logic
  • Data-driven workflows reduce dependency on manual coordination
