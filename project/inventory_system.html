Problem Context

In small and medium-scale manufacturing environments, inventory and procurement decisions are often driven by manual tracking, informal vendor coordination, and short-term urgency rather than structured planning. This creates uncertainty in material availability and makes procurement outcomes highly dependent on individual experience rather than system-level logic.

Key Challenge: Procurement decisions are frequently made without explicitly accounting for demand variability, lead-time uncertainty, and changing market conditions within a unified decision framework.

Project Objective

The objective of this project was to design a decision-oriented workflow that integrates inventory visibility, demand estimation, and procurement timing logic to support reliable manufacturing operations while reducing dependence on ad-hoc coordination.

System Flow & Decision Logic

Step 1: Order Processing

Production Order & BOM Generation

When a production order is initiated, the system generates the required bill of materials (BOM) and compares it against available inventory levels.

Step 2: Demand Analysis

Requirement Estimation

Material requirements are estimated using an EOQ-based framework that balances ordering frequency with holding considerations under practical manufacturing constraints.

Step 3: Market Analysis

Price Trend Evaluation

Market price trends are evaluated to compare immediate procurement with alternative purchasing windows, supporting informed timing decisions.

Step 4: Decision Logic

Procurement Timing Selection

The system prioritizes securing inventory required for ongoing production while allowing flexibility in procurement timing when conditions permit.

Step 5: Execution

Procurement Execution

Procurement actions are initiated based on predefined rules and vendor availability, reducing reliance on manual follow-up.

Step 6: Safety Stock

Buffer Management

Safety stock levels are defined using demand variability and lead-time uncertainty to maintain production continuity.

Why This Matters

In manufacturing operations, procurement timing and coordination have a significant influence on production stability. This project illustrates how structured decision logic can improve consistency and transparency in procurement workflows.

By treating procurement as an engineering decision problem rather than a purely transactional task, the system supports more predictable and explainable operational outcomes.

Key Features

  • BOM-Based Requirement Calculation

    Material requirements derived directly from production orders and current inventory status.

  • Rule-Based Reorder Logic

    Reorder thresholds defined using demand and lead-time considerations.

  • Market-Aware Decision Support

    Procurement timing evaluated under alternative price scenarios.

  • Vendor Lead-Time Tracking

    Structured recording of vendor performance to reduce uncertainty.

  • Decision Visibility

    Clear representation of inventory status and procurement rationale for operational planning.

Key Learnings

Systems Thinking

Manufacturing performance depends on interactions between inventory, procurement, and market dynamics rather than isolated decisions.

Timing Sensitivity

Procurement timing can influence operational stability as much as order quantity.

Decision Transparency

Explicit decision logic improves consistency and explainability in operations.

Risk Awareness

Safety stock must balance uncertainty with practical holding constraints.

Process Discipline

Structured workflows reduce dependence on individual judgment alone.

Vendor Coordination

Systematic coordination improves predictability of procurement outcomes.
