Competition Overview

AeroThon is a national-level aerospace design competition organized by SAE India, focused on developing UAV solutions for disaster-struck environments such as floods and cyclones. The competition emphasizes rapid manufacturability, operational robustness, and real-world deployability rather than purely theoretical performance.

My Role & Leadership

I served as Captain and Mechanical Lead, with responsibility for the overall mechanical architecture, manufacturing strategy, and structural reliability of the UAV. The role required balancing performance goals with manufacturing speed, cost, and ease of repair.

  • Owned end-to-end mechanical design of the UAV platform
  • Defined manufacturing methods and material selection
  • Designed modular and crash-resistant structural layouts
  • Integrated design-for-manufacturing principles to reduce build time

Manufacturing & Operational Focus

The UAV was designed for rapid production and field repair, with emphasis on modular components, simplified joints, and tolerance-robust assembly. The architecture supported operation in light rain and high-wind conditions, enabling repeated missions with minimal downtime.

Results & Recognition

The team secured 1st Place overall at AeroThon 2024 and received the Best Business Proposal award, recognizing both the technical execution and system-level feasibility of the design.
