Competition Overview

RoboNation SUAS is an international UAV competition that evaluates teams on autonomous mission execution, system integration, and operational reliability across repeated mission cycles under real-world constraints.

My Role & Responsibilities

I served as Vice-Captain and Mechanical Lead, with responsibility for manufacturing strategy, structural design, and field-operational readiness. My primary objective was to ensure the UAV could be transported, assembled, repaired, and redeployed efficiently during competition operations.

  • Defined manufacturing workflows and build sequencing
  • Led composite fabrication and structural integration
  • Designed modular interfaces for rapid subsystem replacement
  • Planned assembly, transport, and field-deployment logistics

Key Operational Achievement

The UAV was engineered for rapid field deployment, achieving a complete structural assembly time of under 5 minutes. This capability was enabled through a modular carbon-fiber rod architecture, reduced fastener count, and simplified mechanical interfaces.

Manufacturing & Operations Focus

Design decisions were driven by transport constraints, repeated assembly cycles, and short turnaround times between missions. Emphasis was placed on design-for-manufacturing (DFM), design-for-assembly (DFA), tolerance robustness, and minimizing operational failure points.

  • Modular structure to enable sub-5-minute assembly
  • Reduced joint count to minimize vibration and loosening
  • Structural layouts optimized for repeated disassembly
  • Manufacturing methods chosen for fast iteration and repairability

CAD Design & Structural Layout

The UAV was fully designed in CAD with emphasis on load paths, manufacturability, and interface standardization. Structural joints and mounting interfaces were optimized to reduce part count and simplify assembly.

Carbon Fiber Manufacturing

Carbon-fiber composites were used for the primary structure and aerodynamic canopy to achieve a high stiffness-to-weight ratio while enabling rapid fabrication and on-site repair.

  • Material: Carbon fiber composite
  • Components: Structural rods and aerodynamic canopy
  • Process: Hand layup with mold-based curing
  • Focus: Lightweight, structural rigidity, and fast turnaround

Final UAV & Field Operations

The final UAV configuration was validated through repeated ground testing and field trials, with emphasis on structural reliability, vibration behavior, and maintenance efficiency.
